Study in Japan

Your Step-by-Step Pathway to Japan

Transitioning to Japan requires planning and accuracy. Our team ensures that your application is flawless at every stage, maximizing your Certificate of Eligibility (COE) acceptance rates.

1. Assessment & College Match

We evaluate your educational background and budget, pairing you with premium language schools in Japan.

2. Language Preparation

Students enroll in N5/N4/N3 training to satisfy the mandatory Japanese study hours required by immigration.

3. COE Document Processing

We assemble a complete dossier of educational transcripts, sponsor bank certificates, and submit it to Japanese Immigration.

4. COE Issuance & Tuition Payment

Once the Certificate of Eligibility is issued, you pay your tuition fee directly to the school’s bank account in Japan.

5. Visa Grant & Fly

We submit your COE to the Embassy in Dhaka, secure your visa stamp, and organize pre-departure briefing sessions.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions about studying or working in Japan? Check out our quick answers below or contact our desk directly.

To apply for a Student Visa in Japan, you need to have completed at least 12 years of formal education (HSC or Alim pass in Bangladesh, or equivalent Diplomas). In addition, you must show at least 150 hours of verified Japanese language study (NAT/JLPT N5 level certificate is highly recommended to ensure visa approval).

The total timeline is approximately 5 to 6 months. This includes university selection, compiling financial documents, submitting file sets to Japanese Immigration, waiting for COE approval (takes 2-3 months), paying fees, and securing the visa stamp from the Embassy in Dhaka.

Yes. International students in Japan are legally allowed to work part-time up to 28 hours per week during academic semesters, and up to 40 hours per week during scheduled school vacations. Typical part-time jobs include convenience stores, food preparation, teaching, and delivery networks.

SSW is a direct Japanese working visa category designed for skilled labor. Candidates must pass two exams: the JLPT/NAT N4 language test (or JFT-Basic) and a sector-specific skills Prometric exam (e.g. Caregiving, Food Service, Agriculture). Upon passing, recruiters match you with Japanese companies for full-time contracts.

Our support doesn't end at the airport. We coordinate with the school representatives for airport pickup in Japan, help you locate temporary/student housing, advise on residency registration, and link you with senior alumni networks to guide you on part-time jobs and bank setups.
